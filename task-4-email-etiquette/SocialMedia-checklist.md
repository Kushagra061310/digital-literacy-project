Social Media Dos and Don’ts

✅ The "Dos" (Best Practices)

1. Keep things current. Make sure your LinkedIn profile reflects your latest skills, new certifications like a HackerRank badge, and your recent projects.
2. Use a professional photo. Stick to a clear, high-quality headshot with a plain background. 
3. Join the conversation. Like and comment on posts about AI, web development, or engineering. It’s a great way to grow a network that actually helps your career.
4. Verify your info. Double-check technical news or facts before you share them. You want people to see you as someone who knows what they’re talking about.
5. Watch your privacy settings. Take a look at your Instagram or Facebook every now and then. It’s smart to keep your personal life separate from your work life.

❌ The "Don'ts" (Common Mistakes)

1. Stay professional. Don't post anything offensive or too personal on sites where a recruiter might find you. 
2. Skip the slang. When you're on LinkedIn or messaging a pro, avoid "text-speak" like using "u" for "you." It looks sloppy.
3. Don't share too much. Never post private stuff like university registration numbers or internal lab details online. 
4. Avoid online fights. Getting into heated arguments or leaving mean comments isn't worth it. Your digital footprint sticks around forever.
5. Don't over-tag. Nobody likes spam. Avoid tagging professors or big industry names in posts that don't apply to them just to get views.
