Stay Safe Online Checklist

General Online Safety Tips
1. Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Setting up 2FA for your college email, LinkedIn, and social media is a must. It adds a second layer of protection so a password alone isn't enough to get into your accounts.
2. Create Strong, Unique Passwords: Don't use your name or birthday. Instead, make sure every account has a different password made up of symbols, numbers, and capital letters.
3. Be Careful with Links: Never click on weird links in texts or emails. These phishing scams often try to look like they’re coming from your bank or school, but they're just traps.
4. Keep Your Software Current: Update your phone’s operating system and apps whenever you see a notification. These updates often fix security holes that hackers use to spread malware.
5. Check Your Privacy Settings: Take a few minutes every now and then to look at your settings on Instagram and Facebook. Make sure you know exactly who can see your photos and personal info.
6. Check the Facts: Don't just forward "emergency" alerts or news in WhatsApp groups. Always look for official info before you hit send.

Financial & UPI Safety
7. Your UPI PIN is for Sending Only: You don’t need your PIN to receive money. If someone tells you to type in your PIN to "collect a prize" or "claim a refund," they are trying to rob you. 
8. Double-Check the Receiver: Before you hit "Pay" on a UPI app, look at the name on the screen. Make sure it matches the person you're actually trying to pay so you don't get tricked by a fake QR code.

How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you run into trouble or find yourself facing a threat, use these official resources right away:
• National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
• National Helpline Number: 1930 (This is open 24/7)
